About

I'm currently pursuing my M.S. in Mechanical Engineering and Applied Mechanics (MEAM) at Penn, building on my B.Tech in Production Engineering from NIT Tiruchirappalli (CGPA 8.98/10). My interests lie in design for manufacturing, additive manufacturing, and computational mechanics.

My recent work includes simulation-driven design at Ashok Leyland Technical Centre, advanced thermal modeling of additive manufacturing (LMD) using physics-informed neural networks, and motorsport engineering through the PSI Racing team at NIT Trichy.

Selected Projects

PSI Racing – Suspension & Steering Subsystem

NIT Trichy • Design, validation, manufacturability

Designed and validated components for the BAJA ATV as part of the PSI Racing team — focusing on parts we could actually fabricate in the NIT Trichy workshop. Used PTC Creo for modeling and ANSYS Workbench for structural checks (cornering, steering gear, and knuckle loads) to stay within SAE BAJA safety and performance limits.

Highlights: CAD, FEA, report documentation, vehicle-level validation

Coaxial UAV Motor Mount

CAD (Creo) • Structural design • Lab project

Designed a mount to interface a brushless motor with a carbon tube in a coaxial UAV stack. Prioritized stiffness, weight reduction, and ease of machining/3D printing for rapid iteration.

Physics-Informed Thermal Modeling of LMD

Python • PINN • Advanced manufacturing

Built a physics-informed neural network in PyTorch to predict 3D temperature fields in laser metal deposition, incorporating convection/radiation losses and realistic laser heat inputs. Validated with FEM and benchmark data.

Magnetorheological Clutch – Control & Testing

Western University, Canada • Research

Designed and tested a miniaturized planetary MR clutch to achieve controllable torque transfer. Modeled the clutch, instrumented it with Arduino Mega 2560 for speed/torque acquisition, and compared experimental results with analytical predictions to validate the MR fluid’s response.

Optimization of Wire Arc Additive Manufacturing

Final-year project • NIT Trichy

Simulated the WAAM process in Simufact Welding to minimize residual stresses and distortions across multi-layer bead deposits. Applied Goal Programming for process parameter optimization and validated simulation results with physical prints.

Tools: Simufact Welding, MATLAB, CAD

Gallery — Simulations & Prototypes

BAJA chassis and steering FEA — Von Mises stress distribution for impact and rollover.

Experimental setup for MR clutch — torque and speed acquisition with embedded sensors.
