About

I'm currently pursuing my M.S. in Mechanical Engineering and Applied Mechanics (MEAM) at Penn, building on my B.Tech in Production Engineering from NIT Tiruchirappalli (CGPA 8.98/10). I enjoy the intersection of design for manufacturing, additive processes, and computational modeling.

Recent work includes designing UAV hardware, developing PINN-based thermal models for LMD, and contributing to PSI Racing (suspension & steering subsystem) where the parts actually had to be made and validated.

Selected Projects

PSI Racing – Suspension & Steering Subsystem

NIT Trichy • Design, validation, manufacturability

Worked on the design, validation, and fabrication of the front and rear suspensions of an All-Terrain Vehicle (BAJA SAE). Used PTC Creo and Lotus for design, and ANSYS Workbench for auxiliary checks. Focused on parts we could actually make in-house and on meeting Baja competition standards.

Coaxial UAV Motor Mount

CAD (Creo) • Structural design • Lab project

Designed a mount to interface a brushless motor with a carbon tube in a coaxial UAV stack. Prioritized stiffness, weight reduction, and ease of machining/3D printing so it can be iterated quickly.

Physics-Informed Thermal Modeling of LMD

Python • PINN • Advanced manufacturing

Built a physics-informed neural network in PyTorch to predict 3D temperature fields in laser metal deposition, incorporating convection/radiation losses and realistic laser heat inputs. Compared with FEM and literature (NIST AM-bench style).

Magnetorheological Clutch – Control & Testing

Western University, Canada • Research

Designed and tested a miniaturized planetary MR clutch to transfer variable torque, instrumented with Arduino Mega 2560 to collect speed and torque data. Validated the model against experimental results.

Optimization of Wire Arc Additive Manufacturing

Final-year project • NIT Trichy

Simulated the WAAM process in Simufact Welding to minimize residual stresses and distortions. Validated with experimental trials and used Goal Programming to obtain optimal process windows.
